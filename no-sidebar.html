Queensley Chinedu

Data Scientist | Data Analyst | Statistician

Having graduated with a first-class degree in statistics, I bring strong analytical skills and a knack for hypothesis testing. Before diving into the world of data science, I explored Java development, even publishing an app I developed for my personal use on the Google Store. However, drawn by the potential to merge my statistical prowess with programming, I ventured into data science.

During my internship at the Ministry of Economic Development in Akwa Ibom, I developed models to cluster local government areas based on various metrics, from crime rate to standard of living. Additionally, as a freelance data analyst and machine learning consultant, I've enjoyed dabbling in A/B test experiments, crafting machine learning models, and diving into data analysis and visualization using Python libraries. Plus, I'm well-versed in SQL database design and implementation, collaborating closely with clients to deliver solutions promptly and effectively.

Alongside my technical expertise, I possess excellent communication, planning and time management skills, as evident in my ability to balance work life, personal life and take up several volunteer roles. Furthermore, I'm highly receptive to learning opportunities, always eager to expand my knowledge and skill set in pursuit of personal and professional growth.

Looking ahead, I'm keen to further my career in data science by pursuing a PhD in the field. My ultimate goal is to leverage data science to solve pressing healthcare and agricultural challenges in Africa, making a tangible impact on people's lives and contributing to the region's sustainable development.
